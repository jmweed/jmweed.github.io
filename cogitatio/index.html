Reflection on the Discrete and the Continuous

 

1.

           

The mind is a system. The system rewards certain outcomes and punishes others. Thoughts and the feelings associated with them analogize well with pleasure and pain on the physical level. From the reward of physical pleasure and the punishment of physical pain, our movements evolve organically to account for these factors. Such movements connect with patterns of movement stored in the brain. However, I am not here to describe muscular coordination or the neurological systems involved, mainly because the system works fine for myself and those whom I love.

 

The process of rewards and punishments for various muscular movements (third level), comes from the signals of pain and pleasure (second level), which come from physical contact with the world (first level; electric shock, slap, burn, twisting, painful stuff). With modern technology, these can be fiddled with. Using painkillers, the signal can be numbed to reduce its perception by the third level. So the pain still “exists” at the first or second level, but it is simply not perceived at the third level. Sometimes, this effect is genuinely useless and should simply be medicated with whatever painkiller (e.g., chronic pain, mostly in those of advanced years, which likely cannot be medically fixed, and does not serve much of a function). However, sometimes the pain is extremely functional. In the event of a hangover, the pain experienced is the body’s method of saying: “don’t do that again.”

 

Human motivational structures evolved the same way – we feel good (rush of happiness) when moving towards something we want (large, deliciously baked, chocolate cake), and we feel bad (anxiety) when not moving away from what we don’t want (eaten by lion).  The essence of this inquiry is trying to understand the cognitive processes involved with that ‘something’ – how our brain generates that ‘something,’ how it balances between multiple somethings, etc.

 

My claim is that the ‘somethings’ (hereafter teloi) that our brains decide ‘to go after’ are meant to be discrete objectives, and that these teloi (and the behavior that follows in pursuit of them) are planted from the experience of a mental projection of how a certain scenario would feel. Our brains generate images of how we expect a scenario to be (good: eating delicious cake; bad: eaten by tiger) and such scenario plants the motivation for action in proportion to the expected reward or harm.

 

Unconscious planting of ideas occur from witnessing the actions and emotions of other people. If we see Bjorn surrounded by beautiful women, holding loads of nutritious food, and smiling, we can probably guess that we want to do whatever Bjorn is doing, to work towards super Bjorn. We know monkeys do this. Monkeys can see a video of a snake biting another monkey (whoever recorded that is pretty fucked) and know that they should be afraid of snakes, even though they have never seen one.

 

This conscious planting of ideas can occur if we envision (through the abstract thinking superpower of the human brain) a positive scenario where we are happy after a goal is completed (say, riding around with hot babes in a lambo). Envisioning this scenario (with emphasis on the hot babes, and how the hot babes appreciated all the time and work that went into you getting the lambo, and how the hot babes thought it was hot how no other man has a lambo like you) would likely be truly motivating to go out and get a Lamborghini. Intelligence, however, is the accuracy of these mental projections. If I intentionally envisioned myself doing something that probably wouldn’t make me happy, I wouldn’t be motivated to do it (for some reason, the lambo scenario isn’t doing it for me, maybe because I haven’t thought about it for long enough). However, we are not fully conscious of our own intelligence or inaccuracies (simply impossible, because if we were conscious of the inaccuracies in our mental projections, they wouldn’t exist).

 

            Real evil (most modern marketing / advertising) occurs when one subconsciously plants a telos that seems to be accurate, but is actually inaccurate.  If I see an advertisement for Bjorn Body Spray that is really just spray water in a can, but the advertisement claims that Bjorn is surrounded by beautiful women because of his body spray (a bold faced lie!), that is evil.

 

            Moreover, human psychology is much better (i.e. more effective) when those ‘somethings’ are discrete moments, objects, or accomplishments that are worked towards through continuous labor – completing a level in a video game, buying a lambo, going to the marketplace, etc.

 

            I think most of the projections occur subconsciously, and we follow them through subconscious motivation rather than explicitly realizing what we are doing at any given time. Intelligence is probably not even genetic (maybe it is, idk) it is probably just significantly affected by the consciousness of what one is pursuing and what things someone is able to observe. Sounds like proper education, huh!

           

            Depression (the consideration that a telos should be abandoned) works well in a system where our objectives are discrete.
